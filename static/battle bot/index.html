Challenges Faced:
  1. Servo Mount Strength:
    The torque from the flipper’s 25kg servo caused early 3D-printed mounts to crack. Reinforcing them with thicker walls and brackets fixed the issue.
  2. IR Signal Interference:
    IR receivers occasionally lagged or picked up false signals. Dual receivers and wire shielding improved reliability.
  3. Power Distribution:
    Voltage drops occurred when servos and logic shared power. Splitting power rails stabilized performance.
  4. Flipper Timing Control:
    Fine-tuning angle limits and servo delay logic prevented servo stress and improved snapback precision.
  5. Wiring Management:
    Cramped internals required smart routing with heat shrink and zip ties to reduce interference and mess.
  6. IR Remote Programming:
    Button presses sometimes repeated or lagged. Debouncing and state tracking in code fixed the issues.
  7. Servo Overload Prevention:
    Running multiple servos risked overheating. I added movement limits and cooldowns to prevent burnout.
  8. Weapon Sync Logic:
    Simultaneous weapon use caused brownouts. A short cooldown function between hammer and flipper attacks solved it.
