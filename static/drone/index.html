Drone

The drone is custom-built with a 3D-printed frame designed in Fusion 360. The frame is lightweight and reinforced with carbon fiber tubing for durability. The motor mounts required multiple iterations to withstand vibrations, and a custom adapter was designed to securely attach the propellers to the motors. The landing gear is also carbon fiber, with a removable 3D-printed mounting system for easy transport and repairs. For wire management, a 3D-printed ESC holder keeps the components organized and accessible. The build is still being refined, but it is near completion.

    Frame

    The main part of the drone's frame is 3D-printed. I designed this part and all the other components using Fusion 360, a computer modeling software, and then 3D-printed them. The frame is lightweight, weighing less than a pound. The arms of the drone are made from carbon fiber tubing, which is extremely durable while still being lightweight. To prevent the tubes from spinning inside the 3D-printed frame, I drilled a hole and inserted a screw to secure them.

    Motor to Propeller Adapter

    The propellers I had were not compatible with the motors because there was no way to attach them securely. The motor would spin inside the propeller without actually turning it. To address this issue, I designed a custom adapter that allowed me to mount the propellers to the motors effectively.

    Motor Mount

    For the actual motor mount, it took many iterations of design. When the motor would spin up, it tended to instantly break the mounts, so I had to redesign due to vibrations. To prevent the motor mount from spinning on the carbon fiber tube, I designed it with a screw hole at the points where the bottom of the motor attaches to the 3D model, reinforcing those areas.

    Landing Gear

    For the landing gear, I wanted it to be made from carbon fiber due to its strength, durability, and light weight. I designed these 3D-printed mounts that can easily be removed and attached for transportation purposes or in case the landing gear gets damaged. The mechanism is a two-part design, with the top and bottom parts attached with M3 screws, able to hold a piece of carbon fiber tubing at the bottom. It's not completely finished yet, but I'm almost done with the landing gear.

    Drone ESC Holder

    The ESC (Electronic Speed Controller) has a multitude of wires connecting to various components, such as the four motors and the receiver for the drone. It is also positioned next to the battery. It was important that all the wires are easily accessible in case something goes wrong while maintaining neat wire management. I therefore designed and 3D-printed a holder for it, but I think it still needs some work before it's complete.
